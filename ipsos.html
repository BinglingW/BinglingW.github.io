Design Challenge with Ipsos
Project Overview

Contactless shopping has grown rapidly in the past decade. Yet the rapid growth has illuminated behavioral biases and decision-making changes in consumers, creating a gap between consumer expectations and what companies provide.

In this project, my colleague and I identified the behavioral gaps and suggested the next steps that companies can consider.

Behavioral Sciences

Project Management

Project Report
Project Presentation Slides
Step 1: Define Context and Identify Problem
Method: Desk Research

Through reviewing academic papers and business reports on e-commerce and social commerce, I explored the behavior and decision-making process of contactless shoppers and identified the behavioral barriers consumers had that hindered them from switching to contactless shopping platforms.

Example:

Due to status quo bias, people preferred to stay in their current state instead of changing. Due to this bias, consumers reported to be hesitant to try new shopping platforms or technologies such as social commerce.

Step 2: Explore Deeper Consumer Insights
Method: In-Depth Interview

I created interview guides based on the consumer insights extracted from the secondary research. Then, I conducted 5 in-depth interviews with people in varying age groups.

Example:

My colleague and I asked the interviewees if they were concerned about using new shopping platforms or technologies. One of their largest concerns was that new technologies or platforms probably indicated unclear privacy protection, although they accepted that the platforms they were using now were probably not protecting their privacy as well as they expected.

Step 3: Recommended Possible Solutions
Method: Desk Research | Brainstorming

From the interviews, I identified the behavioral barriers that the interviewees faced. After doing more targeted secondary research to explore whether and how companies have addressed the identified barriers, my colleague and I suggested future research directions and potential solutions to these barriers.

my teammate and I suggested in our report that brands should either confront the privacy issue and publicly address their solutions to create trust with consumers, or develop attractive new functions to increase consumersâ€™ perceived benefits of using new platforms or technologies.
