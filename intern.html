A Pilot Study on Augmented Reality
Project Overview

AR surveys have huge potential in market research as it allows the researchers to track the participants’ in-the-moment experiences.

In this project, I reflected on the use of the AR tool and explored how it can be improved and used in future research. This objective was achieved by designing a study to explore the impact of the shopping environment on consumers’ in-the-moment perceptions about the stores and the products.

Experiment Design
Incorporated AR with traditional survey tools

The participant would go to both Walmart and Target to compelte the study. At each store, the participant would find three products one by one.

The participant would aim her phone camera at the product to take a picture. Then, the participant would view the product through the camera for 5 seconds and rate her current feelings towards the product on the SAM scales while looking at the product.

Measuring Emotions: SAM
Bradley & Lang, 1994

Self Assessment Manikin (SAM) consists of three pictorial scales that each measure a dimension of emotion people have, including valence (sad-happy), arousal (calm-excited), and dominance (controlled-in control).

Since the scales do not contain words, SAM can be used regardless of cultural differences and biases introduced from verbal descriptions.

example: SAM scale on emotional valence
